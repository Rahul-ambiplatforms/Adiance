What are Bullet Cameras?

Bullet cameras are identified by their cylindrical bullet-shell-like shape. The video monitoring cameras are known for their long range and ease of installation. These cameras have gained tremendous popularity and acceptance as they are made explicitly for outdoor surveillance purposes, especially the parking lots, school campuses, vehicles, banks, homes, hospitals, and smart cities.

The reason why most of us go for a security camera is the coverage. We want a third pair of eyes looking over our property – in our presence and absence. Bullet cameras can easily accommodate a larger lens offering you a long-range vision. Moreover, since these cameras deal well with bottleneck areas, they are ideal for exterior and distant object viewing.

3. Easy to Maneuver

A fixed camera that captures only a specific patch is of no use to anyone – unless you know what to look for. 

In a security surveillance system, a camera is only as good as its versatility.  Since not everyone gets the camera viewing angle right the first time, bullet cameras have a movable robotic neck. 4. Waterproof

For an outdoor surveillance camera to be effective, it undoubtedly has to be water-proof, and it has to work correctly in all weather conditions. Most IP 66 bullet cameras can withstand powerful jets of water. Although these cameras are built to withstand the elements, it is still a good idea to provide extra housing to keep them out of reach of intruders.

5. Ease of Installation

One of the main reasons why bullet cameras are a popular choice is their ease of installation. Since bullet cameras come with a mounting handle, they are much easier to install than their other counterparts. Besides, these cameras also allow for easier repositioning of the camera angle – provided the angle is within the range.

6. Visibility

Despite being highly visible and less discreet, bullet cameras are preferred for homeowners mainly because visibility can sometimes also help deter. When you know you are being watched, the chances of you committing mistakes are highly reduced. It is human nature, and a noticeable bullet camera can put off an intruder and stop them in their tracks. Of course, you can also combine these security systems with other solutions such as burglar alarms, door sensors, external lighting systems, and more.

7. Light Reflection

Positioning a security camera outdoors can cause a couple of unexpected problems. One of which is the question of light – or the reflection of light. Images can seem to be washed out when light reflected off a shiny object, such as a car mirror or window, falls on the lens. So, it is essential to point the camera at an angle that doesn’t render the images useless due to light reflection.
