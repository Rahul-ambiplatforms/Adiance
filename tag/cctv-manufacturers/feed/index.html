Top 5 Things to Know Before Starting CCTV Manufacturing

CCTV manufacturing stands for ‘closed-circuit television’ and is popularly known as a video surveillance camera. Whether it’s home, commercial spaces or industries, these cameras are found pretty much everywhere we go.

If you want to start a CCTV manufacturing business, you have made a great choice. The CCTV camera market is expected to grow at 10.3% CAGR and reach 10490 Million USD by 2026. This is quite not a surprise as the need for surveillance is bound to increase in almost all parts of the world.

Thanks to the opportunities it offers, CCTV manufacturing is a lucrative business. However, like any other industry, it also comes with complexities and challenges. This blog post will lay out five crucial things that you cannot afford to miss before you get started with CCTV manufacturing.

Read Also this Relevant Blog:- 5 CCTV Manufacturing Industry Trends You Need to Know

1. It’s Not All About Capturing Video Anymore

For most people, all cameras are the same. They all record the video and store the footage for later use. However, it’s not the case.

In recent years, the CCTV camera business has witnessed massive technological advancements. Not only there are many different types of cameras, but the latest technologies such as artificial intelligence, machine learning, and cloud monitoring have also been integrated with these cameras.

Thus, the first thing you should do is learn all about CCTV cameras and the latest technologies in the space. Without understanding the nuts and bolts of the industry, it’s not possible to have a great CCTV manufacturing business.

2. Different Types of CCTV Camera

Which kind of CCTV camera do you want to manufacture? If you aren’t sure about the answer to this question, you should probably learn about the different types of CCTV cameras used in the market. Let’s see some of the most common types of CCTV cameras being used today.

  • PTZ Camera: PTZ is the short form of ‘Pan, tilt, and zoom.’ These cameras are highly sensitive, and they can detect the motion, sound, and change in heat signature and get activated. Thus, they can detect any potentially suspicious activity and warn human monitors.
  • IR Camera: IR (Infrared) cameras are used for surveillance in darkness. Their infrared capabilities allow us to capture the perfect movement of things through Night Vision Cameras. They offer reliable, clear and sharp images in the nighttime.
  • IP Cameras: IP (internet protocol) cameras, as the name suggests, are the cameras that are connected with mobile phones through a broadband internet connection. These are some of the most commonly used cameras in domestic and commercial spaces.
  • Facial Recognition Cameras: These cameras allow users to identify faces that are difficult to recognize within standard video surveillance technology. They offer remarkable accuracy of over 98.5% on public standard data sets.
  • AI Cameras: As the name implies, these cameras come equipped with artificial intelligence and machine learning capabilities. They come with algorithms that detect unusual motions and offer high definition resolution.

Other CCTV cameras include Thermal cameras, 4G/5G cameras, Wi-Fi cameras and ANPR cameras.

3. AI & IoT are the Present and Future

Today, the biggest CCTV manufacturers invest a lot of money in researching and developing new-gen cameras. Although it’s still the transition phase, we’re witnessing a rapid shift in the CCTV camera industry.

Conventional CCTV cameras need constant human monitoring to ensure the safety of a particular space. This becomes quite a challenge when you have hundreds of cameras to monitor. Moreover, humans can make mistakes, resulting in huge losses and accidents.

AI-powered cameras alleviate this problem as they facilitate machine-based surveillance. Plus, as a part of the internet of things (IoT) network, these cameras are now being synchronized with other devices to offer better security. For example, you can integrate AI cameras with an alarm system at your home to warn you of any suspicious activity.

4. Complex CCTV Manufacturing Process

A lot of things go into manufacturing CCTV cameras. It’s a ten-step process starting with circuit development till packaging. Let’s see the steps involved in the CCTV manufacturing business:

  • Step 1: PCB development
  • Step 2: Raw material purchase
  • Step 3: PCB manufacturing
  • Step 4: SMT process
  • Step 5: IC Programming
  • Step 6: Assembling lens
  • Step 7: Focusing process
  • Step 8: Gluing process
  • Step 9: Quality inspection
  • Step 10: Packaging

5. Industry Standards & Compliance

CCTV cameras deal with sensitive information, and that’s why there are stringent standards that CCTV manufacturers must comply with. There are some country-specific standards, and some are applied worldwide. You must do in-depth research so that your manufacturing process follows all the guidelines and don’t face any problems.

Although CCTV manufacturing is a rewarding business, it involves a lot of complexities on the technical and business side. Thus, one of the most important things you need to do is hire good talents who have been in the industry for quite some time. Their experience and expertise will help you set your business on the right track.

If you are looking for a good CCTV manufacturers and are confused, you are not alone. Many people looking for a good quality CCTV surveillance system face this dilemma, and it’s okay to have it. After all, it’s the first line of defense for your home or workplace security.

Choosing the right CCTV manufacturer is not easy, as you can find them in abundance – from startups to multinational giants. Luckily, you are at the right place at the right time. This blog post will explain five crucial factors you need to consider before finalizing a CCTV manufacturer. Let’s get started!

5 Factors to Consider Before Choosing a CCTV Manufacturers for Surveillance

  1. Assess Your Requirements

The first thing you need to do is assess your requirements. CCTV cameras come in many shapes and sizes. They offer features such as night vision, audio, high definition capability, thermal camera, and many more. That’s why you must first have a clear idea of your needs.

You should ask the below questions to yourself:

  • What or who do I want to put under surveillance? 
  • How big is the area you want to cover?
  • What are the risks for the place I want to put under surveillance? How big is it?
  • Do I only need video or need some other features as well?
  • How many CCTV cameras would be sufficient for my needs?
  • Do I have any special requirements, such as VMS analytics?

Along with these questions, you also must gain an in-depth understanding of the type of security surveillance system you want to be installed. For example, if you have a large warehouse, find out what kind of security systems other companies have. If needed, you can even visit them to get a better idea. Similarly, there are different solutions for homes, small businesses, construction sites, etc.

  1. Reputation & Support

One of the most obvious yet most important things is to verify the CCTV manufacturer’s past work. You can do it by checking reviews on independent websites and social media platforms. Among reviews, you must check how good their post-installation service is. That’s because you are sure to encounter problems at one point or another. If you have a reputable CCTV manufacturer by your side, it can make things easier.

Apart from the apparent installation and support services, you must also check how they fare when storing footage on their cloud systems if they provide such a facility. Video footage is sensitive data, and cyber perpetrators can wreak havoc if they get their hands on it. Thus, make sure the company you choose is well-guarded on all fronts.

  1. Integration with Other Security Systems

Many people believe CCTV cameras to be the only security solution they need. They’re obviously wrong as cameras only provide surveillance, but what about the rest of the things? Modern-day security solutions combine various security systems such as cameras, burglar alarms, access control systems, smart lights, etc.

If you’re looking for a long-term solution, you must go with CCTV cameras that can be integrated with various security solutions. Therefore, you must choose a manufacturer with extensive experience in making such cameras.

  1. Research and Innovation

As we discussed in the previous point, we’re witnessing a rise in the adoption of various security solutions that help protect residential, commercial, and public spaces. Like other spheres, CCTV cameras are also constantly changing as CCTV manufacturers try to provide top-notch security to their customers.

We’re already witnessing companies embracing AI-powered cameras that can trigger warnings without human efforts. Such technologies are set to play a significant role in developing next-gen CCTV cameras. Thus, the companies manufacturing these cameras must be good at research and innovation. A way to find out is to check how many people they have in their research division. The more, the merrier.

  1. Warranty & After Sales Support

CCTV manufacturers provide warranty on their products. Most good companies offer one year warranty on their CCTV cameras. Thus, it must be an essential consideration while selecting a CCTV manufacturer.

Apart from the warranty, you should also go with a company that provides good quality post-sales service for support and maintenance. It’s crucial as you might need their help in case of technical issues or if you decide to move your home or workspace. 

Conclusion

Whether you want to protect your home or professional space, selecting a suitable CCTV manufacturers in usa is one of the most important decisions you will make. A tight security system can thwart criminals and act as solid proof in case of any legal trouble. 

Many see expenses after these cameras as a burden, but they’re investments if you look closely. That’s why we’ve not listed cost as one of the five factors. It is undoubtedly essential but not important enough to compromise on the above five factors.
