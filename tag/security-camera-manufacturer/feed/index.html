Best Commercial Security Camera Manufacturers in the USA

Security Camera Manufacturers came as a boon for all business owners as they play a critical role in ensuring a safe and healthy work environment. However, each workplace is built differently and presents a unique set of challenges. The security cameras aren’t just supposed to look after people or stock, but they’re also used to look after many things. For example, they’re often employed to look after machine health in plants. 

Thus, you need to find suitable surveillance systems for your workplace and implement them to the best to ensure the worth of your investments. This starts with selecting the right USA security camera manufacturers. In this blog post, we’ll have a look at the best commercial security camera manufacturers in the USA.

1. Adiance:

Trusted by companies of different domains and scales, Adiance is one of the USA’s leading players in security camera manufacturing. Their security systems are ISO-certified and ideal for indoor and outdoor usage. With 19 years of experience, Adiance has climbed its way to the top on the back of its Research and Development capabilities. With 3 R&D centres, they’re always sharpening their game.

2. Ezviz:

EZVIZ has made its name as a leading security camera manufacturer globally. Their emphasis and expertise on AI and IoT have helped them stay ahead of their competitors and have been behind the inception of many of their products. These solutions are perfect for commercial usage as they go above and beyond videography.

3. Axis:

Axis is a big name in the security camera market. They have their presence in more than 50 countries and have installed their systems at crucial locations like the City of Houston, Madrid buses, the Moscow Metro, Sydney Airport, etc. With a 24.3% market share, Axis is the most popular name for manufacturing security cameras in the USA.

4. Hanwha Techwin:

Hanwha, a South Korean company, was conceived in 1977 as one of the subsidiary companies of Samsung. Back then, it was called ‘Samsung Technwin’, and later on, it was acquired by Hanhwa. Their products included CCTV (Surveillance) systems, modules, Presenter, FRID, DVR, optical instruments, etc. Hanwha Techwin holds 12.1% of the market share in the security camera industry.

5. Hikvision:

If you’re even a little bit into the security camera industry, you would have heard the name of Hikvision. They’re the biggest manufacturer of security cameras globally, with almost 2 out of every 10 manufactured by them.

Their product catalogue consists of HD analogue cameras, smart IP cameras, DVRs, NVRs, speed domes, video management tools and software suites, alarm systems, encoders and more. Be it education, banking, government, legal or any other industry, Hikvision has its presence almost everywhere.

6. Digital Watchdog:

Founded in 1987, Digital Watchdog is a leading provider for big organizations. Post their inception; they expanded rapidly in America and Asia-Pacific. Currently, they hold a market share of 4.3% within the US.

Their core expertise lies in providing new-gen surveillance systems for commercial spaces. Currently, they’re providing security solutions to 60% of the top 20 global brands. The company offers IP cameras, recorders, network devices, software suites, mobile apps, cloud solutions and more.

7. Bosch:

Bosch, a globally known name, has also worked its way into the security camera industry. As you can guess, it’s one of the oldest manufacturers of CCTV cameras. However, they’ve not left a single stone unturned in technical advancements. They have around 3600 people working in their R&D division alone. They have a 5.7% market share globally and 4.1% in the US.

How to Choose the Best Security Camera for Your Business

You’ve made a great decision to install security cameras at your workplace. However, it doesn’t stop here as you have to select the right one from a pool of options. Here are the things you need to keep in mind before finalizing one for your business.

Price

You must start setting a budget as security cameras are available in a wide price range. You have to choose the most economical one in the long run rather than the cheapest one. You also need to factor in installation and maintenance costs.

Resolution

The cameras have various resolutions, with full HDTV capabilities and resolutions as high as 1280×720 or 1920×1080 in 16:9 format. The frame rate is usually 25/30 and 50/60 fps, with a broader spectrum than standard TV.

Intelligence

It can get complicated if you have to monitor an ample space through video surveillance as it requires a workforce. You may need to employ cameras with Artificial Intelligence capabilities in such cases. These can easily recognize humans, vehicles, and objects to protect your place from unwarranted disruptions.
